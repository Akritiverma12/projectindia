🏞 National Parks of Gujarat 🌿🐅 – Preserving Nature’s Glory 🐅🦜

1️⃣ Gir National Park

📍 Location: Junagadh, Amreli & Gir Somnath districts 🌍 Area: 1,412 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: The only habitat of the Asiatic Lion in the world. 🦁 Fauna: Asiatic lions, leopards, striped hyenas, jackals, crocodiles, and over 300 bird species. 🌿 Flora: Dry deciduous forests, teak, acacia, and banyan trees. 🎯 Attraction: Jeep safaris, Kamleshwar Dam, and the Gir Interpretation Zone (Devalia)

2. Blackbuck National Park (Velavadar)

📍 Location: Bhavnagar district 🌍 Area: 34 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: Home to the largest population of Blackbucks in India. 🦌 Fauna: Blackbucks, Indian wolves, striped hyenas, jackals, and foxes. 🌿 Flora: Grasslands, dry deciduous forests, and saline plains. 🎯 Attraction: Ideal for wildlife photography, birdwatching (harriers, flamingos), and safaris

3. Marine National Park

3. Marine National Park 📍 Location: Jamnagar district (Gulf of Kutch) 🌍 Area: 162 sq. km (protected zone) ⭐ Highlights: India’s first marine national park, famous for coral reefs and marine biodiversity. 🐠 Fauna: Dolphins, sea turtles, octopuses, starfish, jellyfish, and over 70 species of coral. 🌿 Flora: Mangrove forests and seagrass beds. 🎯 Attraction: Scuba diving, snorkeling, and exploring Pirotan Island. Attraction: Perfect for birdwatching, photography, and eco-tourism, with cycle rickshaw safaris.

4. Vansda National Park

📍 Location: Navsari district 🌍 Area: 24 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: A lush tropical forest located in the Western Ghats. 🐘 Fauna: Leopards, four-horned antelopes, pangolins, giant squirrels, and a variety of birds. 🌿 Flora: Teak, bamboo, mango, and flame-of-the-forest trees. 🎯 Attraction: Trekking trails, tribal villages, and conservation efforts. Attraction: Experience the raw beauty of the desert, jeep safaris, and stunning sunset views.

5. Nalsarovar National Park & Bird Sanctuary

📍 Location: Ahmedabad & Surendranagar districts 🌍 Area: 120 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: One of the largest wetland bird sanctuaries in India. 🦩 Fauna: Flamingos, pelicans, herons, spoonbills, cranes, and over 250 migratory bird species. 🌿 Flora: Aquatic plants, lotus, reeds, and wetland vegetation. 🎯 Attraction: Boating in Nalsarovar Lake, birdwatching, and photography.

🦌 6. Wild Ass Sanctuary (Little Rann of Kutch)

📍 Location: Kutch, Surendranagar, Rajkot, Banaskantha 🌍 Area: 4,954 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: Largest wildlife sanctuary in Gujarat, famous for the Indian Wild Ass (Ghudkhur). 🦓 Fauna: Indian Wild Ass, nilgai, chinkara, desert fox, caracal, striped hyena, and over 300 bird species. 🌿 Flora: Sparse vegetation, thorny shrubs, and salt-tolerant grasses. 🎯 Attraction: Jeep safaris, birdwatching (flamingos, pelicans, cranes), and the Rann Utsav.

7.Barda Wildlife Sanctuary

📍 Location: Porbandar & Jamnagar districts 🌍 Area: 192 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: A potential second home for Asiatic Lions outside Gir. 🦁 Fauna: Leopards, hyenas, sloth bears, wild boars, and crocodiles. 🌿 Flora: Dry deciduous forests, acacia, and teak. 🎯 Attraction: Scenic landscapes, trekking trails, and conservation projects.

8.Jessore Sloth Bear Sanctuary

📍 Location: Banaskantha district 🌍 Area: 180 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: Established to protect the sloth bear population. 🐻 Fauna: Sloth bears, leopards, civets, porcupines, and jackals. 🌿 Flora: Dry deciduous forests, teak, and grasslands. 🎯 Attraction: Nature trails, eco-tourism activities, and bear conservation programs. Attraction: Birdwatching paradise with large flocks of migratory birds during winters.

9.Purna Wildlife Sanctuary
📍 Location: Dang district 🌍 Area: 160 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: Dense moist deciduous forests, one of Gujarat’s richest biodiversity zones. 🐘 Fauna: Leopards, macaques, flying squirrels, pangolins, and giant squirrels. 🌿 Flora: Teak, bamboo, flame-of-the-forest, and orchids. 🎯 Attraction: Trekking, birdwatching, and visiting local tribal villages.

10. Gaga Wildlife Sanctuary

📍 Location: Devbhumi Dwarka district 🌍 Area: 332 sq. km ⭐ Highlights: A lesser-known coastal sanctuary with rich birdlife. 🦩 Fauna: Flamingos, pelicans, spotted deer, desert foxes, and jackals. 🌿 Flora: Mangroves, dry thorny shrubs, and grasslands. 🎯 Attraction: Birdwatching, nature photography, and marine conservation.
