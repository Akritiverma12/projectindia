Welcome to Jharkhand

Fairs and Festivals of Jharkhand – A Celebration of Tribal Heritage & Culture

1. Major Festivals

Sarhul (March-April) – Worship of nature and Sal tree by tribal communities.
Karma Puja (Aug-Sep) – Celebrates brotherhood and good harvest.
Sohrai (Oct-Nov) – A post-Diwali festival honoring cattle and agriculture.
Tusu Parab (January) – A harvest festival with decorated Tusu idols.

2. Major Fairs

Shravani Mela (Deoghar) – Pilgrimage fair for Lord Shiva during Shravan month.
Chhath Mela – Sun God worship with fasting and offerings at riverbanks.
Kolhua Mela (Gumla) – Tribal fair honoring Nag Devta.
Kunda Mela (Chatra) – Historic cattle fair with cultural events.

3. Waterfront & River Festivals

Chhath Puja – Celebrated on riverbanks and reservoirs like Dimna Lake, Patratu Dam, and Ganga Ghat , where devotees worship the Sun God.
Sohrai & Karam Festival by Lakes & Rivers – Tribal communities perform rituals near water bodies to honor nature and cattle.
Kundri Mela (Latehar) – A historic fair held near Betla National Park, drawing visitors to scenic riverfronts.
