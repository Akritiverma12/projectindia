🌿🏞️ National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries of Bihar 🐅🦜

🐅 1. Valmiki National Park 🌲

Location: West Champaran District
Area: 898 sq. km
Highlights:
- Only tiger reserve in Bihar.
- Fauna: Tigers, leopards, hyenas, and bison.
- Flora: Moist deciduous forests and grasslands.
- Attraction: Wildlife safaris and eco-tourism.

🦚 2. Bhimbandh Wildlife Sanctuary 🌱

Location: Munger District
Area: 682 sq. km
Highlights:
- Known for its hot springs and dense forests.
- Fauna: Tigers, sloth bears, and wild boars.
- Flora: Sal and bamboo forests.
- Attraction: Eco-tourism and adventure trails.

🐬 3. Vikramshila Dolphin Sanctuary 🌊

Location: Bhagalpur District
Area: 50 km stretch of Ganges
Highlights:
- Protects the endangered Gangetic dolphins.
- Fauna: Dolphins, turtles, and migratory birds.
- Flora: Aquatic vegetation and riparian flora.
- Attraction: Dolphin sightings and boat rides.

🌳 4. Gautam Buddha Wildlife Sanctuary 🦋

Location: Gaya and Aurangabad Districts
Area: 259 sq. km
Highlights:
- Famous for its diverse biodiversity.
- Fauna: Leopards, wild boars, and peacocks.
- Flora: Dry deciduous forests.
- Attraction: Nature trails and birdwatching.

🦅 5. Udaypur Wildlife Sanctuary 🦜

Location: West Champaran District
Area: 8.87 sq. km
Highlights:
- Known for its serene lake and birdwatching.
- Fauna: Migratory birds and aquatic species.
- Flora: Aquatic vegetation.
- Attraction: Popular among bird enthusiasts.

🐆 6. Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary 🌲

Location: Kaimur District
Area: 1,342 sq. km
Highlights:
- Largest sanctuary in Bihar with rich biodiversity.
- Fauna: Tigers, leopards, and sloth bears.
- Flora: Dense forests and medicinal plants.
- Attraction: Adventure trails and safaris.

🦢 7. Kanwar Lake Bird Sanctuary 🦆

Location: Begusarai District
Area: 63.11 sq. km
Highlights:
- Asia’s largest freshwater oxbow lake.
- Fauna: Migratory birds and aquatic species.
- Flora: Aquatic plants and vegetation.
- Attraction: Birdwatching and nature exploration.

🌱 8. Pant Wildlife Sanctuary 🦜

Location: Nalanda District
Area: 70 sq. km
Highlights:
- Established for the preservation of biodiversity.
- Fauna: Peacocks, jackals, and leopards.
- Flora: Mixed deciduous forests.
- Attraction: Eco-tourism and jungle exploration.

🐾 9. Rajgir Wildlife Sanctuary 🌾

Location: Nalanda District
Area: 35 sq. km
Highlights:
- Nestled among the Rajgir hills.
- Fauna: Leopards, hyenas, and wild boars.
- Flora: Dry deciduous forests.
- Attraction: Trekking and sightseeing.

🦢 10. Nagi-Nakti Bird Sanctuary 🐦

Location: Jamui District
Area: 2.1 sq. km
Highlights:
- Important wintering ground for migratory birds.
- Fauna: Cranes, storks, and other avian species.
- Flora: Aquatic plants and lake ecosystem.
- Attraction: Birdwatching paradise.
