History

  • Ancient Period: The region was part of the Mauryan and Satavahana Empires, with a strong cultural presence in Buddhism.
  • Medieval Period: It flourished under the Vijayanagara Empire and later saw rule from the Qutb Shahis and the Mughals.
  • Colonial Era: It was part of the British-controlled Madras Presidency.
  • Post-Independence: Andhra Pradesh was formed in 1953 from Telugu-speaking regions of Madras, and Telangana became a separate state in 2014.

Religion

  • HINDUISM: The dominant religion, with a rich tradition of temples and festivals. Key pilgrimage sites include Tirupati and Srisailam.
  • ISLAM: A significant minority religion, with a notable presence of Muslims in cities like Hyderabad, Vijayawada, and Kurnool.
  • CHRISTIANITY: A growing community, particularly in the coastal regions and areas like Visakhapatnam, with churches and Christian institutions.
  • BUDDHISM: Although historically significant, especially during the Mauryan Empire, Buddhism is now a smaller community but has notable historical sites.
  • JAINISM A small but historically significant community, with temples and followers in parts of the state.
  • OTHER RELIGIONS: Minor communities include Sikhs, Zoroastrians, and indigenous tribal beliefs.

Fair And festivals


  • UGADI: The Telugu New Year, celebrated with prayers, feasts, and cultural events, marking the beginning of the new year according to the lunar calendar.

  • SANKRANTI (MAKAR SANKRANTI): A harvest festival, celebrated with kite flying, bonfires, and special foods like "Pongal" and "tilgul."
  • DIWALI: The festival of lights, celebrated with lighting lamps, bursting crackers, and offering prayers to Lord Rama and Goddess Lakshmi.
  • DASARA (DUSSEHRA): Celebrated to mark the victory of good over evil, with processions, cultural performances, and religious ceremonies, especially in Vijayawada and Bhadrachalam.
  • VINAYAK CHATURTHI: Dedicated to Lord Ganesha, it is celebrated with grand processions, idol installations, and rituals.
  • RAKSHA BANDHAN: A festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters, with the tying of rakhi and exchange of gifts.
  • RAM NAVAMI: Celebrating the birth of Lord Rama, with temple processions and prayers, especially in temples like Bhadrachalam.
  • CHRISHTMAS: Celebrated by the Christian community, with church services, feasts, and community gatherings.
  • MUHARRAM: A significant festival for Muslims, marked by prayers, processions, and remembrance of historical events.
  • BONALU: A traditional festival celebrated in the Telangana region of Andhra Pradesh, where devotees offer food and prayers to Goddess Mahakali.
  • TEJA RAATRI: A festival celebrating the worship of Lord Teja, popular in rural areas, particularly in the Rayalaseema region.

Famous Places

  • Tirupati: Known for the famous Sri Venkateswara Temple, one of the richest and most visited pilgrimage sites in the world.
  • Amaravati: The historical capital city with the Amaravati Buddhist site and the ongoing development of the new capital city.
  • Visakhapatnam: A major port city, known for its beaches like Ramakrishna Beach and Rushikonda Beach, and Kailasagiri hilltop park.
  • Vijayawada: Famous for the Kanaka Durga Temple and the Prakasam Barrage, as well as being an important cultural and commercial center.
  • Srisailam: A popular pilgrimage site, home to the Srisailam Mallikarjuna Temple and situated within the Srisailam Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Kurnool: Known for the Yaganti Temple and the Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Ponduru: Famous for the Ponduru handloom industry, producing some of the finest quality Khadi in India.
  • Bhadrachalam: Famous for the Bhadrachalam Rama Temple, a key pilgrimage destination for Hindus.
  • Lepakshi: Known for its Veerabhadra Temple, famous for its remarkable architecture and hanging pillar.
  • Chilika Lake: One of the largest brackish water lakes in Asia, known for its natural beauty and bird sanctuary.
  • Araku Valley: A scenic hill station in the Eastern Ghats, known for its coffee plantations and tribal culture.
  • Kakinada: Known for its port and the famous Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary.

National Park

    Sri Venkateswara National Park

  • Location: Chittoor District
  • Significance: Known for its scenic beauty and rich flora and fauna. The park is home to the endangered Lion-tailed macaque, various species of birds, and reptiles. It's part of the Eastern Ghats and also houses the famous Tirumala Hills.

    • Rajiv Gandhi (Rameswaram) National Park

  • Location: East Godavari District
  • Significance: Located near the Godavari River, this park is famous for its wetland ecosystem and is home to a variety of birds, especially migratory species. The park is known for saltwater crocodiles and various aquatic animals.

    • Koundinya Wildlife Sanctuary

  • Location: Chittoor District
  • Significance: A sanctuary that is home to many species of wildlife, including the Indian wolf, leopards, and elephants. The sanctuary offers a great environment for bird watching as well.

    • Papikondalu National Park

  • Location:East and West Godavari Districts
  • Significance: This park is situated along the Godavari River and is known for its pristine beauty, dense forests, and steep hills. It's home to a wide range of flora and fauna and offers stunning views along the river.

    • Nallamala Hills (Nallamala Wildlife Sanctuary)

  • Location: kurnool District
  • Significance:A significant reserve for tigers and other wildlife, including the sloth bear and various deer species. The sanctuary is located in the Nallamala Hills, which are rich in biodiversity
