JHARKHAND

🌿 Jharkhand – The Land of Forests & Tribes 🏞️: Jharkhand, formed on 15th November 2000, is known for its rich tribal culture, abundant minerals, and scenic landscapes. The capital is Ranchi, while Jamshedpur is the industrial hub. The state is home to stunning waterfalls like Hundru & Dassam Falls, wildlife sanctuaries like Betla National Park, and spiritual sites like Baidyanath Dham and Parasnath Hill. Jharkhand is a mining powerhouse with vast coal, iron, and uranium reserves, making Jamshedpur a major steel hub.

Best time to visit Jharkhand

The best time to visit Jharkhand is from October to March, when the weather is cool and perfect for exploring waterfalls, national parks, and temples. Winter is ideal for visiting Netarhat, Betla National Park, Hundru Falls, and Parasnath Hill. Monsoon (July–September) enhances the state's greenery, making waterfalls spectacular but travel tricky. Summers (April–June) are hot, best for hill stations like Netarhat. 🌿🏞️

    Jharkhand – The Land of Forests 🌿
 Jharkhand, known as the "Land of Forests," is a state rich in natural beauty, tribal culture, and mineral resources. It is home to dense forests, wildlife sanctuaries, and scenic waterfalls like Hundru Falls and Dassam Falls. The state is famous for Baidyanath Dham, Parasnath Hill, Betla National Park, and vibrant tribal festivals. Ranchi, the capital, blends modernity with nature, while Jamshedpur is India’s first planned industrial city. With its deep-rooted heritage and abundant resources, Jharkhand is a unique blend of tradition and development.
