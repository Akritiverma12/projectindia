National Parks of Jharkhand – Wildlife Havens of the East 🌿🐅

1️⃣ Betla National Park

Location: Latehar District Area: 1,026 sq. km Highlights: One of India’s first national parks to become a tiger reserve under Project Tiger. Fauna: Bengal tigers, elephants, leopards, sloth bears, bison, and over 200 bird species. Flora: Sal forests, bamboo groves, medicinal plants, and waterfalls. Attraction: Jeep safaris, waterfalls, and historical Betla Fort inside the park.
Jharkhand has only one national park, but it boasts rich biodiversity with wildlife sanctuaries like Palamu Tiger Reserve and Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary.

Wildlife Sanctuaries – A Haven for Nature & Wildlife 🌿🦌

1️⃣ Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary 🐘

📍Location: Near Jamshedpur 📏 Area: 195 sq. km 🌟 Highlights: Known for its large elephant population and picturesque landscapes. 🦁 Fauna: Elephants, leopards, barking deer, sloth bears, wild boars, porcupines, and many bird species. 🌳 Flora: Dry deciduous forests with sal, mahua, bamboo, and teak trees. 🎯 Attractions: Trekking and nature trails with panoramic views. Dalma Hilltop offers breathtaking scenery and a Shiva temple. Best time to visit is October to March for wildlife sightings.

2️⃣ Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary 🦌

📍 Location: Hazaribagh District 📏 Area: 186 sq. km 🌟 Highlights: A scenic sanctuary with rolling hills and dense forests. 🦊 Fauna: Leopards, sambar deer, nilgai, chital, wild boars, jackals, and hyenas. 🌿 Flora: Mixed forests with sal, teak, and bamboo groves. 🎯 Attractions: Jeep safaris to explore wildlife in their natural habitat. Watchtowers for spotting animals near waterholes. Best time to visit: November to April.

3️⃣ Koderma Wildlife Sanctuary 🌳

📍 Location: Koderma District 📏 Area: 150 sq. km 🌟 Highlights: A serene forest reserve rich in birdlife and medicinal plants. 🦜 Fauna: Leopards, hyenas, langurs, jungle cats, mongoose, and various reptiles. 🌺 Flora: Lush greenery with sal, teak, bamboo, and rare medicinal herbs. 🎯 Attractions:Ideal for birdwatching and nature walks. Beautiful waterfalls and scenic viewpoints. Best time to visit: October to March.
